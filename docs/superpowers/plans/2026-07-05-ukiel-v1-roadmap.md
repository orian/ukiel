# Ukiel V1 Roadmap

Spec: `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-05-ukiel-design.md`. V1 is split into sequential plans, executed in order (rows 1–7, 9–21, 27–30, 32, 34, 37, and 39 executed so far — per-row status below is authoritative). Each plan produces working, tested software. Detailed plans are written just-in-time (after the previous plan merges) so interfaces referenced are real, not guessed.

**Recommended execution order for the remaining plans: 35 → 40 → 8, then 31 → 22 → 23 → 24** (40 measures the single-catalog gate before pipelines/scale-out; 16/37/39 executed 2026-07-13 — the read-side tail is closed at 0.99× same-bytes; 35 is the last ready read-side row and is independent of 8; 29 executed 2026-07-09; 32 and 15 executed 2026-07-12) (25/26 gated on design decisions; 31 slots before 22 so coalescing rides sealed-aware merge planning; 32 right after 29 so the official suites measure the compacted layout and exist before the read-side tail churns; 33 is written just-in-time when the 1M-tenant claim needs its benchmark). Rationale: 13 (executed 2026-07-09, pulled forward on the 10 GB-tier baselines): the measured read-path bottleneck is the flat ~45–55 ms per-tenant floor — per-file footer reads × fan-out (67–108 files/query at 100M rows, `bench/README.md` §6) — which the footer/metadata cache removes; it was ungated, read-side-only, churn-immune (immutable files, per-path cache — 27/14/29's writer rewrites are a non-event for it), and the loaded bench fixture makes its before/after a one-minute re-run against a recorded baseline; 28 (executed 2026-07-08) hardened 17/18 (grouped probe, partition index, merge cap, default dedup) ahead of 8's backpressure port; 21 first (pulled forward 2026-07-08 — its gates, 20 and ideally 17/18, are all executed): the regimes 17/18 introduced (ladder backlog, backpressure hover, unfinalized partitions, row-28's capped merges) are dark without P2's collector gauges — the design doc's "alarms standing in for hard limits" — issue 0008's revisit trigger depends on them, and the perf plans that follow churn exactly the paths those gauges watch (same empirically-check-what-follows rationale that put 20 before 17/18); 20 (metrics P1) shipped so its gauges empirically check everything after it; 11 built the perf harness/baseline before 17 changed the file population; 17 rewrote the compactor write path that 12/14/15/16/27 build on (landing it first avoided mutual adaptation and re-benchmarking); 18's backpressure now guards the fleet while the perf plans churn; 27 sits immediately before 14 because it births the shared sorting/writer-props module in `ukiel-core` that 14's key-aligned writer then extends (one module, not two) — it lands after 17 so it ports 17's rewritten compactor write path onto that module rather than forcing the already-written 17 to be re-authored, and its correctness fix is latent (no current config trips it, so no urgency pulls it earlier); 30 right after 21 — volume-scale baselines (10 GB ClickBench read suite, Bluesky write-path runs) must predate the write-path churn (27/14/29) and read-path churn (13/15/16) they will measure, the same baseline-before-churn rationale that put 11 before 17, and P2's gauges (21) instrument the runs; 29 sits right after 14 because streaming merge restructures the writer 14 builds and trusts the ordering 27 validates — and it is the PB-scale gate: the plan-28 cap keeps the process alive but leaves 100 GB+ partitions permanently multi-run, so at target scale (100–500 GB partitions/keys are common) the terminal invariant depends on 29, ahead of the remaining read-side tail 15/16 (15 stays after 29 — its prewarm hooks into the `merge_parts` output path 29 restructures); the baselines also strengthen 22 (coalescing shrinks the very fan-out behind 13's floor by collapsing aged day-partitions) but its rewrites want 29's bounded-memory merge underneath, so it keeps its tail slot; 8 executes last because pipelines replace the ingest code 18 and 19 land in (its plan is written, with guardrail-continuity constraints). Rows 21+ are the post-v1 tail: written just-in-time once their gates land. Per-row notes below.
